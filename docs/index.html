The Big Idea

Dancing Pythons is a fun tool for dancing with friends at a party! Our goal was to create a live visual that places a silhouette of you dancing over moving art that is generated based on the mood of the song!

How it works

There are two components to this project: generating art based on the music and developing a live silhouette. The first component mentioned (the generative art) uses mini project 2 and expands it so that the art is only developed using certain colors. The certain colors are determined using Spotify API. Spotify tells us the mood of the song and depending on those values we input certain ranges that the art can use. The generative art also moves to the beat of the music! The second part mentioned (the silhouette) uses Open CV to take in the camera frame and detect edges. It then manipulates these edges so that we have an array of black and white pixels. The white pixels are the trace and the black pixels are the background. Using Open CVâ€™s image addition, we can add the art to the trace. Because a black pixel value is [0,0,0], it does not show up in the final display.

How we got here

We began by creating a minimal viable project. For us, that was getting Open CV to detect edges and produce a trace. Then, we attempted to place the silhouette over art and generate art based on mood. Once we could do this using single images, we developed moving art that corresponded to the tempo of the music playing. Lastly, we worked on placing the sihouette over moving art.
